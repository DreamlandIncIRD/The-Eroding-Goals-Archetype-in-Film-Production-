# The "Eroding Goals" Archetype in Film Production  

[![DOI](https://zenodo.org/badge/DOI/10.5281/zenodo.20052429.svg)](https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.20052429)

## Abstract
This paper examines the "Eroding Goals" archetype in the context of film production. Three scenarios are considered: balanced influence of positive and negative events, increased positive events, and increased negative events.
It is shown that negative events (schedule pressure, budget pressure, compromises) act continuously and, in the long run, outweigh periodic positive events (money injections, community support). The ideal follows film quality with a delay, reflecting a gradual adjustment to declining standards. The most stable quality is achieved when positive events are strengthened; strengthening negative events leads to an uncontrolled drop in film quality and a growing gap between quality and the ideal.
